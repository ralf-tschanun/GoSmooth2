Introduction

GoSmooth2 measures vehicle movement using GPS and the motion sensors built into your iPhone. AI algorithms automatically detect maneuvers and evaluate them based on comfort, safety, and energy efficiency.

In short: The smoother your maneuvers, the higher your score.

More about how GoSmooth2 works

Scores

GoSmooth2 calculates several types of scores that together form your Total Score per trip.

  • Maneuver Scores: Start, Accel, Brake, Stop, Steer
  • Category Scores: Consistency, Safety, Eco
  • Total Score: weighted average of all scores

Introduction

What is GoSmooth2?

GoSmooth2 measures vehicle motion using GPS and built-in iPhone sensors. The app detects maneuvers (Start, Accel, Brake, Stop, Steer) and evaluates them. AI models analyze the motion profile and assign the current driving situation.

Each maneuver receives a Maneuver Score, and categories such as Consistency, Safety, and Eco form your Total Score.

Why smooth driving?

Many drivers believe they are ‚Äúgood drivers‚Äù. Better drivers are those who drive comfortably, safely, and efficiently.

  • anticipate traffic
  • avoid abrupt actions
  • lift the throttle early
  • avoid unnecessary braking forces
  • steer smoothly to avoid weight shifts
  • accelerate and maintain speed steadily

A simple indicator: the passenger‚Äôs head. The less it ‚Äúbobs‚Äù, the smoother the drive.

Fun vs. Reason

Driving can be fun ‚Äî acceleration forces, cornering, dynamics. All fine, as long as you choose the right time and place.

GoSmooth2 helps you strike a balance between fun and everyday efficiency.

Target Audience

GoSmooth2 is for drivers who want to:

Scores & Driving Physics

Basic Evaluation

The smoother your maneuvers, the better the score. Sudden acceleration or braking creates large forces ‚Äî uncomfortable and inefficient.

Acceleration & Energy

Every acceleration requires energy. Braking dissipates energy as heat (combustion cars) or partial regeneration (EVs).

  • accelerate only as much as needed
  • initiate braking early and smoothly
  • maintain speed when possible

Smoothness & Sub-Maneuvers

Evaluated by:

  • number of sub-maneuvers
  • duration
  • changes in g-forces

Steering

Smooth steering means:

  • early, gentle turn-in
  • few corrections
  • steady cornering forces

Technical Details

Sensors & g-Forces

iPhone sensors measure forces relative to gravity.

Drift & Filtering

Long maneuvers may produce sensor drift; GoSmooth compensates where possible.

Normalized Energy Model

Uses simplified assumptions:

  • ~1500 kg vehicle weight
  • ~28% engine efficiency
